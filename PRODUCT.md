# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, and tech leads (India + remote) screening backend developer candidates. They arrive from a CV link or LinkedIn, scan for 20-60 seconds on desktop or phone, and decide whether Helee gets a call. Secondary: freelance clients evaluating credibility.

## Product Purpose

One-page portfolio for Helee Ramani, backend developer (1.5 yrs, Node.js/Express/MongoDB, casino & betting platforms). Goal: convert a skim into an interview call. Success = visitor understands her niche (production casino backends: auth, payments, multi-role access) within one fold and can reach her (email, resume PDF) without friction.

## Brand Personality

Graceful, precise, backstage. Her own line is the brand: "I build the quiet stuff nobody sees but everyone needs." Soft code-editor aesthetic (the site should unmistakably read as designed by a woman who codes — user requirement): a colorful IDE-theme surface (mono coding type, fuchsia primary with mint/peach/periwinkle syntax accents and full-color tech logos) layered over quiet engineering confidence. Structure and precision underneath, warmth and personality on the surface. Emotional goal: trust ("this person ships real production systems") plus personality.

## Anti-references

- Neon-gradient "full-stack ninja" template portfolios (particle backgrounds, skill progress bars, 3D avatars).
- Terminal cosplay: fake CLI intros, `whoami` gimmicks, typewriter "hacking" effects. (Mono type is intentional here; the gimmicks are not.)
- Generic SaaS landing patterns: centered hero + icon-card grids + hero-metric stat tiles.
- Anything that reads "downloaded theme" rather than "designed for her".

## Design Principles

1. **Make the invisible visible** — backend work is the subject; express it through structural precision (grids, hairlines, indices) and small authentic system motifs (request logs, uptime status), not decoration.
2. **Skim-first hierarchy** — name, niche, and contact reachable in one fold; every section scannable in seconds.
3. **Quiet, not timid** — restraint in ornament, commitment in type scale and the crimson accent. Big type is allowed; noise is not.
4. **Real over generic** — concrete production facts (2FA, payment gateways, 6 role panels) beat adjectives.
5. **Content she can edit** — all copy/data lives in one data file so a backend dev can update it without touching layout code.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast on all text. Full keyboard navigability, visible focus states. `prefers-reduced-motion` disables entrance animation and the log ticker. Decorative motifs are `aria-hidden`. Semantic landmarks + heading order for screen readers.
